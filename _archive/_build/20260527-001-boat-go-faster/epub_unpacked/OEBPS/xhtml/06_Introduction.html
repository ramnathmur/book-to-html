Introduction

Would you like to make your life easier? Happier? Would you like to get success more quickly and smoothly? I’d like to think I’m open to fresh ideas, but I’ve always been sceptical of using sporting strategies in everyday life. If someone is psycho enough to train all day, every day for years on end – and all for just the glimmer of a chance of winning Wimbledon, or whatever – then good luck to them, but I’m not a machine.

Then I met Ben Hunt-Davis. Put aside the fact that he is a tad on the tall side and you’d never guess he was a sportsman, let alone a smartie-pants Olympic gold medal winner. He is a good laugh, he makes mistakes, he can be shy, lazy, an idiot, he loses his temper; in other words he is a straightforward and very normal guy, which got me fascinated. How can an ordinary person get such extraordinary results? Is it just luck? Is it sports-specific geeky stuff? Or are there secrets I can steal from his experiences to get more out of my life?

This book is the answer. The crew had bucket-loads of techniques that are easy to understand and easy to apply.

Over the last ten years Ben and I have coached thousands of individuals and we’ve found the techniques described in this book invaluable for helping ourselves and our clients unlock our potential and live happier, more successful lives. Our clients come from all walks of life – call centre staff, sales reps, leadership teams, mums, athletes, team managers, accountants, shop assistants. Whatever your challenges, whatever your goals, there are strategies here that will give you a big boost.

How this book works

We’ve divided the book up into eleven chapters. Each chapter is divided into two halves:

A narrative bit. Here Ben recounts a particular episode on the eight’s journey to Gold. Ben has an uncanny knack for making you feel as though you are there in the boat with them, experiencing what it’s like to be part of an Olympic team.

An analysis bit. This is where I, Harriet, explain why and how the crew did what they did. These sections are packed with step-by-step, practical advice and real life examples to help you apply the team’s strategies in your world.

This is a warts and all, authentic account. Memory is a slippery eel and we’ve done all we can to check back that Ben has remembered things clearly, but at the end of the day it is our analysis. It’s not an exhaustive description of each topic; our aim is to pick out the key points that made the biggest difference and are easy to transfer to different contexts. The topics also intertwine – beliefs influence habits, habits influence beliefs – life is messy and this is as clear as we can get it!
