## Evaluation & Recommendations

_Dated 2026-06-21 (build: The Body Keeps the Score — Bessel van der Kolk)._

**Signal-to-noise:** The best result in the log to date — a 138k-word, evidence-dense expository book condensed to a 56.6k-word, 22-section chaptered reader (41% of body, the high end appropriate for `deep`) that scored a clean **5/5/5/5/5** with source-blind **10/10** comprehension. Bridge ratio is an excellent 2.0% (lowest non-zero in the log), meaning the artifact is almost entirely verbatim van der Kolk joined by minimal connective tissue — the Reader noted this reads at times as quote-after-quote, but on gripping clinical-science prose that's a feature, not a defect. The 20 Channel-A graphics (8 mermaid system/sequence flows, 8 comparison tables, 2 timelines, 1 hand-built SVG window-of-tolerance, 1 EMDR sequenceDiagram) map directly onto the comprehension questions, and `would_skip_original` is true.

**Process notes (sharding economics + three deterministic catches):** (1) **Editor sharding paid off but is fragile** — 5 concurrent shards over disjoint chapter ranges, but **2 shards (B, D) died mid-response on an API connection drop**, each having written only its first chapter. Recovery was cheap because the partials were *complete for the chapter they finished* — kept them and re-edited only the 6 gap chapters as 4 smaller shards. **Lesson: shard at ≤2-chapter granularity on 130k-word+ books to bound timeout blast radius; always verify each partial's last source_id against the chapter's corpus max before trusting it.** (2) **Anchor sweep false-positives** — 3/674 anchors failed rapidfuzz<95, all from corpus footnote-numeral contamination (`questions14`→`questions`) + curly-quote tokenization; the Proofreader correctly cleared all 3 as `pass_on_review`. (3) **One genuine bridge catch** — an EMDR bridge described the finger-tracking mechanic but cited only the two adjacent paragraphs; resolved deterministically by adding the paragraph (ch15§2¶6) that contains the exact mechanic — a citation-widen, not a rewrite. **The shipped `scripts/assemble.py` is stale** (targets the old flat-`<h2>` template + dead slot names); a v3.0 assembler was written to emit the per-chapter `<section class="view chapter">` contract.

**Recommended additional fields:** (1) `shard_count` + `shard_failures` — sharding is now the default for long books and its failure rate (2/5 here) is a real operational signal. (2) `bridge_fix_kind` (`citation_widen` vs `rewrite`) — 1 widen / 0 rewrite again confirms the Editor under-cites rather than fabricates (third build showing this pattern). (3) `anchor_false_positive_count` — footnote-numeral contamination trips the deterministic sweep predictably; tracking it would justify a corpus footnote-stripping pass. Not worth capturing: per-span in/out token split (harness still doesn't expose it); true end-to-end wall for concurrent+timed-out-shard builds is unrecoverable.

_Dated 2026-06-20 (build: 1929 — Andrew Ross Sorkin)._

**Signal-to-noise:** Strong for a 158k-word narrative history compressed to a 44k-word, 8-view reader (39% of the 113k-word body). The Reader scored source-blind 10/10 comprehension — every figure survived intact (the $1B→$6B margin growth, Mitchell's secret $12M/71,000-share rescue, the $120M→$250M bankers' pool, Whitney's $205 U.S. Steel bid, Glass-Steagall's $2,500 deposit insurance over Glass's own objection). Bridge ratio 4.16% sits comfortably mid-band. The honest dock is `books_finished_rate` 4/5: at this depth the artifact is verbatim-anchor-heavy (~90%+ of body is stacked Sorkin blockquotes joined by thin one-line bridges), so it reads as a superb excerpt anthology more than a propulsive narrative — for emotional momentum the original still wins, but for working knowledge of what happened in 1929–33 it clears the gate decisively and `would_skip_original` is true.

**Process notes (resume + two deterministic catches):** (1) **Cross-session resume** — a prior same-day session died after Editor+bridge-rework but *before* Proofreader re-verification (no HTML/verdict/row). Rather than re-run a ~$20-40 Editor, the resume reused the deterministic corpus + reworked edit_pack and re-gated from scratch; the fresh full Proofreader found only 6 minor ungrounded specifics, and **5 of 6 were resolved by citation-widening against text already present in the source** — i.e. the original edit was substantively right but under-cited, vindicating the resume-over-rebuild call. (2) **Two assembler-tier defects caught and fixed deterministically:** the Reader flagged 2 shard-merge duplicate anchors (deduped, re-verified 551/551 verbatim), and a pre-ship audit found that 5 empty-source pure-transition bridges would be silently dropped by the chapter-grouping logic → added carry-forward chapter assignment so all 71 bridges render. **Lesson: chapterless (empty-source_ids) bridges need an explicit chapter inheritance rule or they vanish in chaptered assembly.**

**Recommended additional fields:** (1) `resumed_from_session` boolean + `stage_resumed_at` — this is the second cross-session build in the log (How to Know a Person was the first) and resume economics are now a recurring pattern worth flagging. (2) `bridge_fix_kind` split (`citation_widen` vs `rewrite`) — here 5 widen / 1 rewrite, a strong signal that the Editor under-cites rather than fabricates. (3) `empty_source_bridge_count` — a leading indicator of the silent-drop risk above. Not worth capturing: per-span input/output token split (harness still doesn't expose it); prior-session uninstrumented spans make a true end-to-end wall figure unrecoverable for resumed builds.

Retention is lean at 7% (5,570 words) — correct for `narrative_nonfic` + `standard` (Retention Mass 506 × 11), where the value is the named frameworks plus one vivid anchor each, not the cumulative anecdote texture. Bridge ratio ran hot at 9.96%, right against the 10% ceiling (densest in the log) — worth watching the Editor isn't paraphrasing toward the cap.

**Process notes (two real failure modes caught):** (1) **Stale-draft race** — the Editor kept rebalancing chapter coverage *after* its file first appeared on disk, so the first Proofreader audited an 84-anchor intermediate while the final was 85. Caught by diffing the completion notification's metrics against the sampled file; re-ran the Proofreader on the settled file. **Lesson: never sample an Editor output until its agent reports at-rest.** (2) **Genuine grounding fail** — Proofreader iter1 flagged 12 `bridge_unsupported_claim`s: all citation-address bugs (source_ids pointing at wrong/non-existent §/¶ spans, e.g. ch3§4 when only 3 sections exist), zero fabricated content, all 85 anchors clean. Surgical rework re-cited all 12 (no prose change); re-proofread passed 12/12.

**Output-path correction (important):** this build was first written to the skill's hardcoded scratch path `C:\Claude Code\CLAUDE OUTPUTS\book-to-html\` and then **relocated here to the project** per the global CLAUDE.md project-output rule. The skill's `SKILL.md` hardcodes that scratch path and should be updated to target this project (`books/` + `build-logs/`). The two run-logs have **diverged** — this project's JSONL is missing the HBR Soft Skills build and (until now) listed "Why Machines Learn" in the `.md` table but never in the JSONL; the scratch tree is missing "Why Machines Learn". A one-time reconciliation pass is recommended.

**Recommended additional fields:** (1) `output_path_canonical` boolean — flags whether a build landed in the project vs a scratch tree. (2) `bridge_violation_kind` — separate `citation_address` from `fabricated_content` (they route re-cite vs rewrite). (3) `paused_on_quota` boolean. Not worth capturing: per-span input/output token split (harness doesn't expose it).

_Dated 2026-06-15 (build: Why Machines Learn: The Elegant Maths Behind Modern AI)._

**Signal-to-noise:** Exceptional for expository math-heavy nonfiction. All 14 sections covered; 338 anchor blockquotes carry verbatim source text (governing equations + named results + mechanism explanations). Every major ML algorithm from perceptron to double descent has its governing equation present as a clean inline SVG — not as a reference but inline in the narrative where the concept is introduced. Retention at 27% (~30K/~110K source words) is consistent with prior expository builds (Thinking Fast and Slow: 28%). Bridge ratio 3.2% is well under the 10% ceiling; the book's technical density naturally forces anchor-heavy editing.

**Illustration strength (Channel A only):** 31 inline SVGs (20 math equations + 11 architecture diagrams), all hand-built vector, all self-contained in the HTML. Equation SVGs use Cambria Math in tinted inset blocks with fraction bars and sub/superscripts — superior to raster PNG replacements. Architecture diagrams (RGC→simple→complex cell hierarchy, convolution kernel slide, Prewitt kernels, neuron-as-convolution, CNN pipeline, max pooling, LeNet, bias-variance U-curve, double-descent curve) genuinely externalize the structural concepts. Illustration scored 4/5 (not 5) only because two formula terms in the Gamma function unit (u_0155) were left as broken external raster refs — caught by the Reader and fixed post-gate by the orchestrator with inline unicode math spans. A minor and quickly addressed defect.

**Reader verdict:** Rare 5/5 `books_finished_rate` — the Reader would skip the 300-page original entirely. Comprehension 5/5: all 10 test questions answered from the artifact alone, including the full convergence proof, Bayes' theorem, SVM dual, Hopfield attractor dynamics, universal approximation non-guarantees, and double-descent theory. Navigation 5/5: grouped sticky TOC (Foundations/Classical ML/Networks/Deep Learning) + progress bar + scroll-spy + per-chapter reading times. Visual pleasure 5/5: 66ch humanist serif, dark-mode pair, drop-caps, burnt-orange accent, equation inset blocks — polished for long math-dense reads.

**G1 JSONL truncation (process note):** The original G1 Editor agent ran successfully and produced 82 units, but the JSONL task-notification line was truncated to only the tail 18 units. Root cause: the 103KB+ notification exceeded some per-line threshold in the JSONL writer, losing the first 64 units. Mitigation: source EPUB files were still in temp workspace, enabling a clean G1 re-run (370s wall, 131K tokens). Future mitigation: for large outputs, Editor agents should write their payload to a build folder file in addition to returning it inline — the `G1_editor_handoff.json` convention adopted here is the right pattern.

**Recommended additional fields for run-log schema:** Add `g1_rerun: true` flag for builds that required targeted group re-runs. The current `iters` field tracks full pipeline iterations; targeted re-runs at the group level are a distinct (and cheaper) recovery pattern worth tracking separately.

**Signal-to-noise:** High for a compact self-help parable. At 16,240 source words this is the smallest measured source in the log. The SLII framework is dense for its length: three skills, four D-levels, four S-styles, a matching rule, a three-part performance loop, and six conversation types are all present in verbatim anchor language. Result: 6% verbatim retention (950 words from 16,240) — the lowest coverage percentage in the log, but correct for this category. The parable's narrative scaffolding (dialogue, character development, story beats) scores ≤ 0 on the self_help rubric and was cut cleanly. No chapter was starved; every chapter contributed to its proportional share.

**Rework pattern (iter-1 fail, iter-2 pass):** Proofreader iter-1 flagged two issues simultaneously: (1) a spurious leading `"` in u_0030's `verbatim_quote` field — present in metadata but not in the source paragraph — and (2) a bridge_ratio of 0.16 against the 0.10 ceiling. Editor rework corrected both in a targeted 5-unit pass (42-second wall time, 37k tokens); Proofreader iter-2 passed clean. The bridge_ratio discrepancy (Editor estimated 0.08, Proofreader measured 0.16) arose from a denominator ambiguity: the Proofreader divided bridge words by anchor words rather than total words. The rework resolved it by dropping well below the absolute ceiling. **Schema note:** standardize `bridge_ratio` denominator as `bridge_words / (bridge_words + anchor_words)` across all future builds to prevent this ambiguity recurrence.

**Reader verdict:** 4/5 `books_finished_rate` (clear PASS); comprehension 9.5/10 (Q7 confidence-vs-commitment distinction only partially crisp). Illustration was the strongest dimension (5/5): 8 Mermaid diagrams for a 2×2 system — S1–S4 quadrant, D1→D4 with regression arrows, D→S matching map, SMART flow, performance-management loop, six-conversations tree — carry structural load the prose cannot. Channel-A-only was correct for a 2×2 model. Lowest dimension is visual_pleasure (4): thin chapters with one bridge + one anchor read as a slide outline rather than narrative flow; sec-2 and sec-17 flagged as too sparse for their headings.

**Recommended additional fields:** Nothing new — the existing schema captured this build cleanly. The `ingest_method` / `anchor_verify_pass_pct` fields recommended after Row 8 remain applicable here: ingest was generic-epub (21 chapters, no calibre fragmentation issues); anchor pass was 36/36 verbatim after rework. The wall_clock note remains useful: this build's ~15.5 min compute figure is clean (sequential single-chain pipeline, no concurrent fan-out) — unlike the concurrency-inflated span sums in rows 7–8.
